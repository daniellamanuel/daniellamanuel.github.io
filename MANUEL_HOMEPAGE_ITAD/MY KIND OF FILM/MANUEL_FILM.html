TV Show

The Good Place

This show premeried on September 19, 2016 and ended on January 30, 2020. It is a television series by Michael Schur. The show has a total of 4 seasons and 53 episodes. The story of the show goes like this, it is about a woman named Eleanor Shellstrop (Kristen Bell) who was welcomed after death to "the Good Place", it is a place with a highly selective Heaven-like utopia designed and run by afterlife architect Michael (Ted Danson) as a reward for her righteous life. However, she realizes that it was a mistake that she was sent there and she tried to hide her imperfect past behavior while trying to become a better person.

The Good Place is one of my favorite TV shows because it is not just about comedy and whatnot, it actually teaches you a lot of lesson in real life. It opened my eyes on some issues especially to my personal life. Apart from the numerous lessons, the story if also fun and makes you want to watch more and become curious as to what will happen next. It has a good storyline, the dialogues are amazing, and the portrayal actors are perfect for their characters.

The Uncanny Counter

The Uncanny Counter is a South Korean tv show based from a webtoon 'Daum'. It was first aired on November 28, 2020 and ended on January 24, 2021. It has 16 episodes and rumor has it that it will have a second season. The actors are Jo Byunggyu, Kim Sejeong, Yoo Junsang, and Yeom Hyeran. The show is about So Mun, who is a disabled high school boy who is enlisted to be part of the Counters. They are a group of paranormal-hunters who would search and fight the evil spirits that escapes from afterlife to prey on humans.

This show got me hooked up from the start. It is so entertaining and got me on my feet. As I watch every episode, I always want more because the actors had great chemistry and it is not just about action where they will catch an evil spirit. It also portrays about life and how we could still have fun or joke around even though there are problems. It also showcases amazing team work, friendship, and loving your family. The storyline is good and I hope they would be able to have season two.

Brooklyn Nine-Nine

This show is an American police procedural comedy show created by Dan Goor and Michael Schur. Brooklyn Nine-Nine first aired on September 17, 2013. It will end on its eight season but the release is pushed back due to the pandemic. The television series revolves around Jake Peralta (Andy Samberg), a talented but immature detective in Brooklyn's fictional 99th precint. Jake often comes into conflict with his commanding officer Captain Raymond Holt (Andre Braugher). The rest of the cast features Stephanie Beatriz as Rosa Diaz, Terry Crews as Terry Jeffords, Melissa Fumero as Amy Santiago, Joe Lo Truglio as Charles Boyle, Chelsea Peretti as Gina Linetti, Dirk Blocker as Michael Hitchcock, and Joel McKinnon Miller as Norm Scully.

Brooklyn Nine-Nine have become my go-to-tv show because it's light and funny. Perfect for any day you just want to have a good laugh. The story every episode connects but still has its own twist and differs from other episode if you get what I mean. The characters are also amazing. Despite it being a comedy show, it still features social issues which is good in my opinion. The dialogues are perfect. The creators are really amazing. I'm just sad that it will only have eight seasons, but nevertheless, it is still an amazing show for me.

Hospital Playlist

Hospital Playlist is a South Korean television series first aired on March 12, 2020 and ended on May 28, 2020. The main casts are Jo Jungsuk, Yoo Yeonseok, Jung Kyungho, Kim Daemyung, and Jeon Mido. The second season is set to air in May 2021. The story of the show is simple. It revolves about the story of doctor and nurses who are working in Yulje Medical Center. It focuses on five doctors who have been friends since they entered medical school in 1999.

A drama were I could rewatch it all over again and still enjoy. I love the friendship of the five main characters in this show. Despite having different preferences, personality, and schedule, they still find time supporting and hanging out with each other. I'm glad that it will have a second season because I want more and I know the story could still expand. It showcases the important roles of the people in the medical field. Also shows different types of coping mechanism and life story of different patients. I really love this show and would highly recommend it.

Step Up Movies

This is an American dance film multi-franchise created by Duane Adler. It has five films.

  • Step Up (2006)

    • Tyler Gage (Channing Tatum) receives the opportunity of a lifetime after vandalizing a performing arts school, gaining him the chance to earn a scholarship and dance with an up-and-coming dancer, Nora Clark (Jenna Dewan).

  • Step Up 2: The Streets (2008)

    • Romantic sparks occur between two dance students (Andie and Chase) from very different backgrounds at the Maryland School of the Arts.

  • Step Up 3D (2010)

    • A tight-knit group of New York City street dancers find themselves pitted against the world's best hip hop dancers in a high-stakes showdown.

  • Step Up Revolution (2012)

    • A group of flash mob dancers led by Sean (Ryan Guzman) and the daughter of a hotel tycoon, Emily (Kathryn McCormick), attempt to save a Miami strip populated by a tight-knit community from being developed into hotels.

  • Step Up: All In (2014)

    • All-stars from the previous Step Up installments come together in glittering Las Vegas battling for a victory that could define their dreams and their careers.

Ever since I was a kid, I have always love dancing and having a movie franchise like this is like heaven to me. Although sometimes the movie revolves around romance between the two main characters, I also love the friendship and team work that the characters shows in the movie. As someone who was once part of a dance group, I know the feeling and could relate to the movie. I would also like to praise the dance routines they show. It's the highlight of every movie and each movie has this theme which is perfect.

Spirited Away

This is a Japanese animated fantasy movie written and directed by Hayao Miyazaki. The release date of Spirited Away was on July 20, 2001. The movie is about the story of Chihiro, a 10 year old girl who moved into a new neighbourhood, enters the worl of Kami (spirits of Japanese Shinto folklore). Her parents become pigs by the witch named Yubaba. Chihiro takes a job working in Yubaba's bathhouse to find a way to free her parents and herself so that they would be able to return to the human world.

When I watched this video I was in completely awe and amazed how good it was. The storyline and all the events that happened are great. I would really recommend this video to anyone who haven't watch it. It's just so good I can't say anything else.

White Chicks

White Chicks is an American comedy film directed by Keenen Ivory Wayans. It was first released in the United States on June 23, 2004. The story is about two black FBI agents who go undercover using a whiteface as women to solve a kidnapping plot. The two main actors are Shawn Wayans and Maron Wayans.

This movie is my go-to-movie if I want to have fun. It's really good and the actors are perfect for who they portray to be. The story is for sure common but despite being it like that, they still added funny events which I could remember and still laugh even though I watched it already multiple times.

The Half of It

This is an American coming-of-age comedy-drama movie written and directed by Alice Wu. It was released in Netflix on May 1, 2020. The Half of It stars Leah Lewis, Alexxis Lemire, Daniel Diemer, and Chollin Chou. The movie is about a friendless straight A student Ellie Chu who was approached by an inarticulate football player, Paul Munsky to write a love letter to Aster Flores. At first, Ellie refuses, but accepts it in the end when she found out that their electricity would be cut off if they don't pay. The two connect over their shared interests in literature and art, though Aster believes she is communicating with Paul.

I rarely watch coming-of-age movies but this in particular caught my attention. Although the movie contains some cliche events, I still liked it since watching, I felt the sincerity in every moment. As someone part of the LGBTQ+ community, this is a light hearted film that I think everyone could watch and relate to in some ways. The actors are also good and the dialogues are amazing. I really like Alice Wu's films.
